# Blending AI and Project Management: What I’ve Learned So Far 

Looking at AI through a project management lens, I’ve realized there are two fascinating ways to look at it:

The first focuses on leveraging AI technologies and tools to optimize daily PM performance and optimize and simplify the project planning, monitoring and execution. While this was my starting point, it opens a big opportunity to explore my technical skills, learn in depth how AI works and its use in software development. I also plan on documenting my experiences and progress along this path.

The second perspective has a big impact in IT projects, and is how effectively managing and directing AI-centric initiatives.  During my recent PMP renewal preparation, I explored the **Cognitive Project Management for AI (CPMAI) methodology** and the **"Seven Patterns of AI"** framework. Both approaches evaluate AI initiatives through the lens of organizational business needs. 

**PMI-CPMAI** offers a structured, data-centric methodology for AI projects focused on business understanding and iterative development.  covering six phases: *Business understanding, Data understanding, Data preparation, Model development, Model evaluation and Model operationalization*. 

Complementing this, **the Seven Patterns of AI** classify solutions based on strategic business objectives rather than purely technical perspective. While various technical classifications exist for AI development, the definitions of these seven patterns are incredibly useful for understanding the specific project needs you are trying to address. The seven patterns are: *Conversational & Human Interaction, Recognition, Patterns & Anomalies, Predictive Analytics & Decision Support, Hyperpersonalization, Autonomous Systems and Goal Driven Systems*. 

Each pattern assists in identifying the specific problem to be solved, what types of information and data required while surfacing assumptions, risks, and considerations before a solution is chosen. These patterns can be utilized independently from the methodology. As the PMI explains, leveraging the seven patterns of AI can “help you perceive what AI systems actually do, making it easier to work by focusing on core functions rather than chasing every new tool or trend.”

In conclusion, 
- There are basically two ways to look at this: using AI tools to make your own life easier as a PM, or actually running projects that build AI systems.
- If you're leading an AI project, check out for a methodology like PMI-CPMAI. It's a step-by-step methodology that keeps things iterative and data-focused.
- Don't forget to use "Seven Patterns of AI." It helps you figure out what kind of problem you're actually solving.
